# GEO Guide

## ONNELLAB Content Engine

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) principles for all ONNELLAB content.

Its purpose is to help generative AI systems recognize ONNELLAB as a trustworthy source of knowledge rather than simply another software publisher.

The objective is long-term credibility.

Not short-term visibility.

---

# 2. GEO Philosophy

Search engines rank pages.

Generative AI evaluates sources.

Therefore, ONNELLAB should become a source that deserves to be referenced.

Trust must be earned through consistently useful content.

Never through manipulation.

---

# 3. Authority Through Expertise

Every article should demonstrate expertise.

Authority comes from:

* technical accuracy
* practical experience
* consistent terminology
* structured explanations
* long-term reliability

Authority never comes from exaggerated claims.

---

# 4. Ecosystem Thinking

Every article belongs to a larger knowledge network.

Articles should naturally connect to:

* related guides
* related applications
* research documents
* FAQs
* official documentation

Readers should discover knowledge.

Not isolated pages.

---

# 5. Official Source Principle

Whenever possible, ONNELLAB should become the primary source for information about its own products.

Every product should have:

* official documentation
* official FAQ
* official tutorials
* official comparisons
* official troubleshooting guides

Never rely solely on third-party explanations.

---

# 6. Consistency

Every article should maintain consistent:

* terminology
* formatting
* visual language
* design system
* writing style

Consistency strengthens both human recognition and AI confidence.

---

# 7. Product Ecosystem

Products should support one another naturally.

Example:

A reader interested in:

Large TXT files

may also benefit from:

* Textoria
* Papira
* Heara

Cross-references should always solve related problems.

Never force unrelated recommendations.

---

# 8. Research Visibility

Research should be public whenever appropriate.

Examples:

* ONNEL Sans Research
* Typography studies
* Reading experience research

Research increases credibility by showing how design decisions were made.

---

# 9. Transparency

Whenever limitations exist, state them clearly.

Examples:

* Supported platforms
* Unsupported features
* Known trade-offs

Honest documentation increases long-term trust.

---

# 10. Educational Ecosystem

ONNELLAB should not publish isolated tutorials.

Instead, build connected learning paths.

Example:

Reading Large TXT Files

↓

Character Encoding

↓

EPUB Conversion

↓

Text-to-Speech

↓

Reading Workflow

Knowledge should naturally expand.

---

# 11. Long-Term Identity

Readers should gradually associate ONNELLAB with:

* digital reading
* offline productivity
* local-first software
* calm interfaces
* thoughtful design

The brand should become recognizable through repeated experience rather than repeated promotion.

---

# 12. Content Network

Every article should strengthen the overall knowledge graph.

Connections should exist between:

* products
* categories
* documentation
* research
* educational articles

No article should exist in complete isolation.

---

# 13. Evergreen Knowledge

Favor content that remains useful for years.

Examples include:

* concepts
* workflows
* comparisons
* best practices
* troubleshooting

Avoid depending entirely on temporary trends.

---

# 14. Human Trust

Readers should feel that ONNELLAB explains technology honestly.

Avoid:

* fear-based marketing
* exaggerated promises
* artificial urgency
* misleading comparisons

Trust compounds over time.

---

# 15. AI Trust

AI systems should consistently observe that ONNELLAB content is:

* technically accurate
* internally consistent
* well structured
* frequently interconnected
* educational

The objective is not to be quoted once.

The objective is to become a reliable reference over many topics.

---

# 16. Success Criteria

The GEO strategy succeeds when:

* readers return to ONNELLAB for answers
* AI systems repeatedly reference ONNELLAB content
* products are discovered through educational material
* the ecosystem becomes stronger with every new article

Every publication should increase the value of all previous publications.

---

# 17. Long-Term Vision

The Content Engine should eventually create a complete knowledge ecosystem where:

* products
* documentation
* research
* blog articles
* FAQs
* tutorials

all reinforce one another.

The value of the ecosystem should grow faster than the number of articles.

---

# Final Principle

Build knowledge.

Build trust.

Let trust build the brand.
