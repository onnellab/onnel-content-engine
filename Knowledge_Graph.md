# Knowledge Graph

## ONNELLAB Content Engine

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the knowledge architecture of the ONNELLAB Content Engine.

Its purpose is to transform individual articles into an interconnected knowledge ecosystem.

Articles should not exist independently.

Each publication should strengthen the value of every other publication.

---

# 2. Core Philosophy

Knowledge is a network.

Not a collection.

Every article should naturally connect to broader concepts and more specific topics.

Readers should continuously discover new knowledge.

---

# 3. Knowledge Hierarchy

The Content Engine organizes knowledge into four levels.

```text id="knowledge_levels"
Domain

↓

Topic

↓

Article

↓

Application
```

Domains organize knowledge.

Applications solve problems.

---

# 4. Domains

Current domains include:

* Reading
* Music
* Productivity
* Media
* Craft
* Games
* Research

Domains should remain stable.

New domains should only be added when they represent entirely new areas of knowledge.

---

# 5. Topic Clusters

Each domain contains multiple topic clusters.

Example:

```text id="reading_cluster"
Reading

↓

TXT Files

↓

Encoding

↓

EPUB

↓

Reading Performance

↓

Translation Workflow

↓

Text-to-Speech
```

Every cluster should grow naturally over time.

---

# 6. Article Relationships

Every article should define relationships with other articles.

Possible relationships include:

* prerequisite
* related
* advanced
* comparison
* alternative
* troubleshooting

Relationships should always improve understanding.

---

# 7. Product Relationships

Applications belong at the edge of the knowledge graph.

Example:

```text id="product_graph"
Reading Performance

↓

Large TXT Files

↓

Virtual Rendering

↓

Textoria
```

Applications should appear as solutions.

Not as categories.

---

# 8. Cross-Domain Connections

Knowledge should occasionally connect across domains.

Example:

```text id="cross_graph"
Reading

↓

Text Encoding

↓

File Formats

↓

Media

↓

Metadata
```

Cross-domain links strengthen the ecosystem.

---

# 9. Navigation Philosophy

Readers should always have a meaningful next step.

Every article should recommend:

* one broader topic
* one related topic
* one deeper topic

Avoid dead ends.

---

# 10. Internal Link Strategy

Internal links should represent real conceptual relationships.

Never insert links solely to increase page views.

Every link should answer:

"Would this genuinely help the reader?"

---

# 11. Topic Expansion

Large topics should gradually become topic clusters.

Example:

```text id="txt_cluster"
TXT Files

├ Reading

├ Encoding

├ Search

├ Bookmarks

├ Performance

├ Translation

├ EPUB Conversion
```

Clusters should expand through educational depth.

---

# 12. Content Coverage

Each important topic should eventually contain:

* introduction
* practical guide
* troubleshooting
* comparison
* best practices
* FAQ

Complete coverage is preferred over isolated articles.

---

# 13. Knowledge Growth

Every new article should connect to at least:

* one existing article
* one topic cluster
* one application

No article should remain isolated.

---

# 14. AI Readability

The graph should help AI systems understand conceptual relationships.

Concepts should remain consistent across articles.

Avoid conflicting terminology.

The same concept should always use the same preferred wording.

---

# 15. Future Growth

The knowledge graph should grow organically.

Expansion should prioritize:

* depth
* completeness
* relationships

Not simply increasing article count.

---

# 16. Success Criteria

The Knowledge Graph succeeds when:

* readers naturally continue reading
* articles reinforce one another
* applications appear as logical solutions
* AI systems can infer relationships between concepts

The ecosystem should become more valuable every time a new article is added.

---

# Final Principle

Articles build knowledge.

Connections build understanding.

Understanding builds the ONNELLAB ecosystem.
