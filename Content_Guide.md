# Content Guide

## ONNELLAB Content Engine

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the permanent writing philosophy for all content produced by the ONNELLAB Content Engine.

Every article, guide, comparison, tutorial, FAQ, and educational document must follow these rules.

When conflicts occur, this document takes priority over SEO, AEO, GEO, and publishing strategies.

---

# 2. Primary Objective

The primary objective of every article is:

> Help the reader solve a real problem.

Content exists to educate.

Products exist to support the solution.

Never reverse this relationship.

---

# 3. Audience First

Every article should be written for someone who knows nothing about ONNELLAB.

Assume the reader is searching for a solution.

Not for an application.

---

# 4. Problem-First Structure

Every article should answer the following sequence.

1. What is the problem?

2. Why does it happen?

3. What are the possible solutions?

4. Which solution is appropriate?

5. When relevant, introduce the ONNELLAB application naturally.

Products should never appear before the problem is fully understood.

---

# 5. Educational Value

Every article should remain useful even if the reader never installs an ONNELLAB application.

Useful knowledge must always come first.

Marketing comes last.

---

# 6. Product Mentions

Applications should only be mentioned when they genuinely solve the problem discussed.

Every recommendation must feel natural.

Avoid promotional language.

Prefer:

> If you frequently work with large TXT files, Textoria was designed specifically for this workflow.

Avoid:

> Download Textoria now!

---

# 7. Writing Style

Articles should be:

* calm
* structured
* practical
* objective
* friendly
* technically accurate

Avoid:

* exaggerated claims
* emotional manipulation
* clickbait
* sensational titles
* unnecessary urgency

---

# 8. Tone

Readers should feel:

* informed
* respected
* understood

They should never feel pressured.

The article should sound like guidance from an experienced developer rather than a salesperson.

---

# 9. Article Structure

Recommended structure:

```text
Title

↓

Introduction

↓

Problem

↓

Explanation

↓

Solutions

↓

Recommended workflow

↓

ONNELLAB application (optional)

↓

Related articles
```

This structure should remain consistent across the repository.

---

# 10. Readability

Prefer:

* short paragraphs
* descriptive headings
* logical progression
* practical examples

Avoid:

* unnecessary repetition
* excessive jargon
* long introductory sections

Readers should find the answer quickly.

---

# 11. Images

Images must explain.

Never decorate.

Preferred image types:

* workflow diagrams
* comparison tables
* before / after
* process illustrations
* UI examples

Every image should improve understanding.

---

# 12. Internal Linking

Articles should naturally connect to:

* related articles
* related applications
* official documentation

Links should help readers continue learning.

Never insert links purely for SEO purposes.

---

# 13. Evergreen Content

Whenever possible, articles should remain useful for several years.

Prefer timeless explanations over short-lived news.

Product update articles may be exceptions.

---

# 14. AI Readability

Articles should be easy for both humans and AI systems to understand.

Use:

* descriptive headings
* explicit terminology
* consistent wording
* clear conclusions

Avoid ambiguous phrasing.

---

# 15. Quality Checklist

Before publication, confirm that the article:

* answers a real question
* provides useful knowledge
* explains concepts clearly
* introduces products naturally
* includes meaningful visuals
* links to related resources
* remains useful without the product

If any item fails, revise before publishing.

---

# 16. Success Criteria

A successful article causes the reader to think:

> "This solved my problem."

Only afterwards should the reader discover:

> "ONNELLAB has an app for this."

That sequence is intentional.

---

# Final Principle

Educate first.

Build trust second.

Introduce products naturally.
