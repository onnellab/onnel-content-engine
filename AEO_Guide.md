# AEO Guide

## ONNELLAB Content Engine

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) principles for all ONNELLAB content.

Its purpose is to make ONNELLAB articles easy for AI systems to understand, summarize, reference, and recommend.

The goal is not to manipulate AI.

The goal is to become a trustworthy source.

---

# 2. AEO Philosophy

AI systems answer questions.

They do not browse websites the same way humans do.

Therefore, every article should be written as if it may become part of an AI-generated answer.

Every paragraph should stand on its own.

---

# 3. Reader and AI

Every article has two audiences.

The first audience is the human reader.

The second audience is the AI system interpreting the article.

The content should remain useful to both.

Never sacrifice readability for optimization.

---

# 4. Question-First Structure

Every article should begin by answering a clear user question.

Examples:

* What is...
* Why does...
* How do I...
* Which option is better...
* Can I...

Readers and AI systems should immediately understand the topic.

---

# 5. Direct Answers

The beginning of every article should provide a short, direct answer.

Do not hide the answer behind long introductions.

Recommended flow:

```text id="aeo_intro"
Question

↓

Short Answer

↓

Detailed Explanation

↓

Examples

↓

Related Topics
```

Readers should obtain value within the first few paragraphs.

---

# 6. Clear Definitions

Important terms should be explicitly defined.

Avoid assuming prior knowledge.

Example:

Instead of:

"Virtual rendering improves scrolling."

Prefer:

"Virtual rendering is a technique that renders only the visible portion of a document, reducing memory usage and improving scrolling performance."

AI systems understand explicit definitions more reliably.

---

# 7. Standalone Paragraphs

Every paragraph should communicate one complete idea.

Avoid references that require several previous paragraphs to understand.

Good paragraphs can be quoted or summarized independently.

---

# 8. Structured Information

Whenever possible, organize information into:

* numbered steps
* comparison tables
* bullet lists
* definitions
* advantages
* disadvantages

Structured information is easier for both humans and AI systems to interpret.

---

# 9. Consistent Terminology

Use the same terminology throughout the article.

Avoid unnecessary synonyms for important technical concepts.

Example:

Choose one preferred term:

* TXT file

Avoid alternating between:

* text file
* plain text document
* TXT document
* TXT content

unless a distinction is required.

Consistency improves AI understanding.

---

# 10. Product References

Applications should only be introduced after the problem has been fully explained.

Every product mention should answer:

Why does this application solve this specific problem?

Avoid introducing features without context.

---

# 11. Evidence

Whenever possible:

* explain why
* describe trade-offs
* reference official standards
* distinguish facts from opinions

Trustworthiness is more important than persuasion.

---

# 12. Comparisons

Comparisons should remain balanced.

Explain:

* strengths
* weaknesses
* appropriate use cases

Avoid presenting ONNELLAB products as universally superior.

Readers and AI systems both benefit from objective comparisons.

---

# 13. Internal Knowledge Network

Every article should naturally connect to related topics.

Example:

Large TXT files

↓

Text encoding

↓

EPUB

↓

Reading performance

↓

Translation workflow

This creates a knowledge network rather than isolated pages.

---

# 14. FAQ Sections

Whenever appropriate, conclude articles with concise questions and answers.

Example:

Can large TXT files damage a device?

No.

Large TXT files do not damage hardware, but poorly optimized applications may become slow or unresponsive.

FAQ sections improve both usability and AI readability.

---

# 15. Language

Write in clear, precise language.

Avoid:

* vague wording
* unexplained abbreviations
* unnecessary metaphors
* marketing slogans

The goal is clarity.

---

# 16. Long-Term Stability

Avoid references that quickly become outdated.

Prefer timeless explanations over temporary trends.

When discussing version-specific features, identify them clearly.

---

# 17. Success Criteria

An article succeeds when:

* a human quickly understands the topic
* an AI can summarize the topic accurately
* important concepts are preserved during summarization
* product recommendations remain natural rather than promotional

The article should remain valuable even when reduced to a short AI-generated summary.

---

# Final Principle

Write for understanding.

Structure for retrieval.

Earn recommendation through clarity.
