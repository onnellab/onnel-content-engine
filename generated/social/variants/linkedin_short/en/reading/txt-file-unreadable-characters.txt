Broken-looking TXT files are often an encoding problem, not a content problem.

The useful test is to keep the original file unchanged, open a copy, and check the encoding before assuming the content is damaged.

- Keep the original TXT file unchanged.
- Open a copy in a reader or editor that lets you choose encoding.
