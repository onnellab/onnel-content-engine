# Topic Guide

## ONNELLAB Content Engine

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the permanent topic selection rules for the ONNELLAB Content Engine.

The purpose is to ensure that every published article contributes to the long-term growth of the ONNELLAB knowledge ecosystem.

Topics are strategic assets.

Every topic should remain valuable long after publication.

---

# 2. Core Philosophy

Content begins with questions.

Not products.

Every article must answer a real question that someone is likely to ask.

The application should become the answer.

Never the starting point.

---

# 3. Topic Sources

Topics may originate from:

* Frequently asked questions
* Real user problems
* Product documentation
* Product limitations
* Comparisons
* Tutorials
* Troubleshooting
* Best practices
* Common misconceptions

Topics should never exist solely to advertise a product.

---

# 4. Evergreen Priority

Whenever possible, prioritize evergreen topics.

Examples:

* How to...
* Why...
* Best practices
* Common mistakes
* Comparisons
* Definitions

Avoid topics that become obsolete quickly unless documenting product updates.

---

# 5. User Intent Categories

Every topic should belong to one primary search intent.

## Learn

The reader wants to understand.

Examples:

* What is FLAC?
* What is EXIF?
* What is UTF-8?

---

## Solve

The reader has a problem.

Examples:

* TXT file won't open
* MP3 tags are missing
* EPUB formatting looks wrong

---

## Compare

The reader is choosing between options.

Examples:

* TXT vs EPUB
* WAV vs FLAC
* Local player vs streaming

---

## Workflow

The reader wants a better process.

Examples:

* Organize a music library
* Read large TXT files
* Convert TXT to EPUB

---

## Discover

The reader is looking for recommendations.

Examples:

* Best offline music player
* Best MP3 tag editor
* Best clipboard manager

---

# 6. Topic Quality Rules

A topic should satisfy all of the following.

* Solves a real problem.
* Remains useful over time.
* Can be explained objectively.
* Naturally relates to one or more ONNELLAB products.
* Fits an existing content category.

If any condition fails, reconsider the topic.

---

# 7. Product Mapping

A single topic may support multiple applications.

Example:

```text id="topic_map"
How to organize MP3 metadata

↓

TagWeaver

↓

Melivra
```

Products should never compete within an article.

They should support one another.

---

# 8. Category Mapping

Topics belong to problem domains.

Current domains:

* Reading
* Music
* Productivity
* Media
* Craft
* Games
* Research

Categories describe problems.

Applications solve them.

---

# 9. Duplicate Policy

Do not publish duplicate articles.

Instead:

* update the existing article
* expand it
* create a more specific follow-up article

Every article should have a clear purpose.

---

# 10. Topic Lifecycle

Every topic passes through the following states.

```text id="topic_flow"
Idea

↓

Approved

↓

Research

↓

Draft

↓

Review

↓

Published

↓

Updated

↓

Archived (rare)
```

Topics should remain traceable throughout their lifecycle.

---

# 11. Topic Priority

Prioritize topics using the following order.

1. Frequently asked user questions
2. Common technical problems
3. Educational guides
4. Workflow improvements
5. Product comparisons
6. Feature announcements

Educational value always takes priority over promotion.

---

# 12. Topic Database

Every topic should contain at least:

* ID
* Status
* Category
* Related applications
* Primary language
* Priority
* Primary question
* Primary keyword
* Publication status

This information should remain independent of the article itself.

---

# 13. Success Criteria

A successful topic leads to an article that:

* remains useful for years
* naturally connects to other articles
* supports multiple ONNELLAB products when appropriate
* strengthens the overall knowledge ecosystem

---

# 14. Long-Term Vision

The topic database should eventually contain hundreds of interconnected ideas.

The value of the Content Engine should grow because every new topic strengthens existing content.

The objective is not to publish more articles.

The objective is to build a richer knowledge network.

---

# Final Principle

Questions create topics.

Topics create knowledge.

Knowledge builds the ONNELLAB ecosystem.
